Explore San Miguel, Bohol

A prime eco-tourism destination with progressive community and rich heritage

Eco-Tourism Destination

Part of our municipality's vision

San Miguel, Bohol envisions itself as "a prime agro-industrial center and eco-tourism destination with progressive, disaster resilient community, dynamic governance in a healthy and safe environment."

Located in the northeastern part of Bohol province, San Miguel offers visitors a glimpse into authentic rural Boholano life, agricultural heritage, and warm hospitality. The municipality's fertile lands along the Cambangay River have attracted settlers for generations.

With 18 barangays and a thriving population of over 23,000 residents, San Miguel welcomes tourists who seek off-the-beaten-path experiences away from the crowded tourist spots.

Agro-Industrial Heritage

Fertile Agricultural Lands

San Miguel is known as a prime agro-industrial center. The fertile lands along the Cambangay River have attracted settlers for generations, making agriculture the backbone of the local economy.

Visitors can experience authentic farm-to-table experiences and witness traditional farming practices passed down through generations.

Cambangay River

The municipality's original name "Cambangay" originated from a weed called "bangay" that thrived along the Cambangay River. This river has been central to the town's history and development.

The river provides irrigation for agricultural lands and serves as a natural attraction for eco-tourists.

Historical Significance

The Story of the Three Miguels

San Miguel was named after three important figures:

Saint Michael the Archangel

The Patron Saint

Miguel Cambangay

Political Bigwig

Miguel Cresino

Stalwart & Brave Native

On March 14, 1961, President Carlos P. Garcia signed Executive Order No. 423 creating barrio Cambangay Sur into a municipality, changing its name to San Miguel in honor of these three Miguels.
